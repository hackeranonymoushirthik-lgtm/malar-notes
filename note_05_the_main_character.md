# Note 05 — *The Main Character*

**To:** Malar — the protagonist. The lead. The one the whole story is really about.
**From:** Hirthik Balaji
**Subject:** Why you are not a supporting character in anyone's story — including your own.

---

> *"En samayal pathi theriyum oru nal — apo papom."*
> — You, leaving a door open to every future good thing.

In Tamil cinema — and I know you love this world as much as I do, maybe more — there is a particular kind of character that steals every scene but never gets the title card. The friend, the confidante, the one whose advice changes the hero's fate. The one who shows up with the right word at the right time and then stands at the edge of the frame while the hero takes the credit.

I need you to understand something: **you are not that character.**

You are the *main character*. In your own story — obviously, always. But also, Malar, in mine. Not the sidekick. Not the supporting arc. The *lead*. The person who, when someone writes the honest story of how Hirthik Balaji became who he is, takes up the most pages and gets the best lines and whose absence would collapse the entire narrative.

This note is your title card. Finally, officially, undeniably: *Malar — Main Character.*

---

## Part One: *What Makes a Main Character*

Let's talk about what it actually means to be a main character. Not in the meme sense — not the "I walked into a coffee shop and everyone stared" kind of main character energy. But in the real, literary sense.

A main character is the one around whom other things organise themselves. The one whose growth is the plot. The one whose presence creates meaning. The one whose choices, wisdom, warmth, or honesty *changes things* for everyone else in the story.

By every one of those criteria — you.

Your growth IS the plot. Over the years I've known you, I have watched you evolve with a steadiness that is genuinely beautiful to witness. From the girl who was carrying the grief of a love story quietly, trying to make sense of her own heart — to the person who knows her worth so completely that she can say *"I deserve to be happy"* without flinching. From someone who was learning how to have boundaries to someone who can hold her ground with grace. From the student figuring out her path to the woman who walks it with intention.

That arc — that specific, hard-won, gorgeous arc — is *the story*.

---

## Part Two: *The Scenes That Prove It*

Every main character has defining scenes. The moments where the audience leans in and thinks: *there it is — there's who she really is.*

Here are some of yours.

**The scene where you chose dignity over bitterness.** When everything had gone wrong with someone you'd loved truly and deeply, you made the single hardest choice a heartbroken person can make: you protected his dignity while holding yours. You said you would never speak badly of him. You said you would pray for his happiness. And then you turned toward your own life and said: *I deserve to be happy too.* That scene? That is a main character's scene. That is where the audience understands that this person is operating at a different level.

**The scene where you showed up for me anyway.** There were times when you were going through your own storms. Times when the pressure was immense and the uncertainty was real and you had every right to be completely absorbed in your own concerns. And in those times, you still asked. You still checked in. You still made space for me even while you were navigating your own chaos. That is not a supporting character move. That is someone who loves people as a *practice*, not just a reaction.

**The scene where you held your ground.** When someone — anyone — underestimated you, dismissed you, or didn't give you the minimum you deserved, you didn't dissolve. You got sad sometimes, yes. You felt it, yes. But you didn't lose your sense of your own worth. You processed it and you *stood*. You kept standing. That kind of quiet unbreakability? Main character. All the way.

**The scene where you made me laugh at exactly the right moment.** I don't want to only talk about the heavy things. Because you are not a heavy person — you are someone who carries heavy things *lightly*, which is entirely different. You have a wit that is *devastatingly* sharp. You have made me laugh at moments when laughter felt almost impossible. You've thrown in a perfectly timed *"lol enga"* or a reference to something we both find ridiculous and just — completely disarmed whatever tension was building. Comedy is an underrated superpower. You wield it brilliantly.

---

## Part Three: *On the Way You Move Through the World*

Main characters have a particular quality to the way they move through life. They're not passive. They're not waiting for the story to happen to them. They engage. They show up. They make choices.

You move through the world that way.

You are curious — genuinely curious about people, about what makes them who they are, about what they're going through. You ask real questions and you want real answers. You're not making conversation; you're making *connection*. That is an active thing. That is a choice you make every time.

You are honest — not in a blunt, weaponised way, but in the way of someone who respects people enough to tell them the truth. Who believes that real relationships require real exchanges. Who knows that pretending is exhausting and that the people worth having in your life will appreciate the truth.

You are brave — in the specific way that the most important bravery always manifests: not dramatically, but quietly. In the everyday choices to stay open when closing would be easier. To trust when wariness would be understandable. To hope when cynicism would be more defensible.

These qualities — curiosity, honesty, bravery — are not incidental to who you are. They *are* who you are. And they are precisely the qualities that make you the protagonist of any story you inhabit.

---

## Part Four: *Why You Are the Main Character of My Story Specifically*

I have said this in several different ways across several different notes, but I want to say it one more time with full clarity and zero ambiguity:

**The most important parts of who I am were shaped by you.**

Not entirely — I have other people who have mattered, other experiences that formed me. But you are woven through the foundational moments of my becoming in a way that no honest account of my life could omit. You were there when I was figuring out what real friendship looked like. You were there when I needed to be told the truth about myself. You were there when the noise was loudest. You were there when I needed someone to believe in me more than I believed in myself.

When people in films have a person like that — the one who *made* them — there's always a scene where they acknowledge it. Where they look at the person and say: *because of you.* 

This is that scene.

**Because of you, I know what it looks like to love people well.** Because of you, I know what honesty within friendship feels like. Because of you, I know that strength can be soft and softness can be strong. Because of you, I know that peace is possible on the other side of pain. Because of you, I laugh more, trust more, hope more.

The main character of a story is not always the loudest person in the room. Sometimes they're the one sitting beside you, quietly, asking if you're okay, handing you the courage you didn't know you needed. Sometimes they're the one who loves you so specifically and so truly that their love becomes part of the architecture of who you are.

**That is you. That has always been you.**

---

## Part Five: *The Title Card You Were Always Owed*

Here it is, Malar. The title card. The official declaration. The thing I should have said a hundred different ways a hundred different times but never managed to put together quite like this:

**You are the main character.**

Not because you demand attention — you've never demanded anything. Not because you perform for the audience — you've never performed a day in your life. But because of *who you actually are*, in the quiet moments, in the unscripted parts, in the everyday choices that add up to something remarkable.

You are the main character because you love without conditions and forgive without keeping score. Because you speak the truth even when it costs you something. Because you hold people in their worst moments without needing them to be grateful in any particular way. Because you are funny and warm and *real* in a world that is very often none of those things.

You are the main character of my story. You are the main character of your own. You are the person without whom the plot doesn't make sense, the warmth without which the scenes are cold, the voice without which the whole thing is quieter and less.

And wherever the story goes next — whatever chapters are still unwritten, whatever plot twists are still ahead — I know one thing with complete certainty:

**The best scenes will be the ones with you in them.**

Thank you for being my main character, Malar. Thank you for letting me be a part of yours.

---

*— Hirthik Balaji, forever your writer, your audience, your person.*

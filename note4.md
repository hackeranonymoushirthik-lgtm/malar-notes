Do you remember the wheat dosa? I need to tell you about this in full because it's one of my absolute favourite memories of being me. It was a regular night, I was hungry, and there was absolutely nothing in the house to eat. No dosa batter. Nothing. So I did what any sensible person in a cooking crisis would do — I took wheat flour, added water, mixed it into some kind of suspicious batter, and made dosas out of it. Wheat dosas, Malar. I ate them proudly. And the next morning I told you, fully expecting applause, and you laughed so hard at me. You called me a losu. You said I was the only person in the world who would innovate dosa in a moment of domestic crisis. And you were right. But here's the thing — I was proud of those dosas. And the fact that you laughed that hard about something so silly made that entire night worth it. That laugh of yours, Malar. Even through text, I could feel it.

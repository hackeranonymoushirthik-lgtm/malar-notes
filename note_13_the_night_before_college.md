# Note 13 — *The Night Before College*

**To:** Malar — who sent me off into the next chapter like only she could.
**From:** Hirthik Balaji
**Subject:** September 1st, 2024. The night before everything changed. And what you did with it.

---

> *"Nalaiki oru puthu manithanaaaaa elumbuuu...ela kootayum alichitu start as freshhhh oh freshhhh...as new clg boiiii"*
> — You, at midnight, making sure I walked into my first day with your love trailing behind me like a second shadow.

I want to tell you about a night.

Not a dramatic night — there were no crises, no emergencies, nothing unusual from the outside. Just the eve of something. The quiet, charged, slightly terrifying eve of a new beginning. My first day of college was the next morning. The hostel was new. The faces would be new. The city I'd be navigating would be new in the way that all cities feel new when you have to exist in them independently for the first time. And I was — if I am being honest — genuinely nervous. Excited, yes. But underneath the excitement, quietly vibrating with the particular anxiety of not knowing what was coming.

And then there was you.

You didn't just wish me luck that night, Malar. You *sent me off*. And I want to hold that difference still for a moment, because it matters. Wishing someone luck is a transaction — it's brief, warm, good-natured, over in a sentence. What you did that night was something else entirely. It was *long*. It was *specific*. It was layered with everything you knew about me and everything you believed I was capable of, delivered with the full force of everything you felt.

---

## Part One: *What You Said*

I am going to return to your exact words — not because I have them memorised (though I do), but because I want you to understand what they did to me in real time.

*"Vera lvl chapter of ur life ha ithu iruka pothu unaku... Nala bayangaramaa enjoyyy pana poraaa... unaku pidichathaa inom Nala semaya padika pora... hostel life umm semayaa iruka pothu naraya good souls ha meet pani 4 yrs avangaloda iruka pora jolyyyu haa... elathukummmmm my hearttyyyyyuuy all d besttttt... kadavul kudutha clg ithu unakuuu elam nalathe nadakum."*

Read that again. Slowly.

You said *kadavul kudutha clg ithu unaku* — that this college was given to him by God. You weren't just congratulating me on an admission. You were *consecrating* the moment. You were saying: this is yours, rightfully and completely yours, and the universe put it here for you. That kind of belief — that warm, spiritual, specific belief in someone's deservingness — lands differently than ordinary encouragement. It lands in the place where the anxiety lives and says: *you belong here.*

And then — because you were never someone who stayed in the abstract when the personal would do more — you brought it home:

*"Nanu yapayum un kudavaeeeee irupen... also my only besttt friend who is in my cityyyyy ofc athu Chennai nu namala emathinalum u r the only one near soo yaaaa."*

That sentence. That one sentence. *My only best friend in my city.* You said it so casually, so matter-of-factly, as if it were simply a geographical fact — but underneath it was the full weight of what we were to each other. The realness of it. The specific, particular nature of the bond between us. Chennai could try to separate us, time could try to complicate us, life could try to pull us in different directions — but you were still here. Still mine. Still saying *u r the only one near.*

*"Nanu ena than irunthalum una romba romba rombaaaaaaaaaaaa misss panuven."*

And then you made me cry a little, which I will deny to anyone else but will admit freely to you.

---

## Part Two: *What That Moment Was*

That night, Malar, you gave me something that I took with me into every new classroom, every new hostel corridor, every awkward first conversation with a stranger: **the certainty of being loved by someone who knew me completely.**

That is not a small thing to carry into an unknown place.

When you step into a new world — new people, new systems, new ways of being — the question that runs quietly under everything else is: *who am I here?* When no one knows your history, no one knows your story, no one knows the specific texture of who you are — you have to rebuild yourself from scratch in the eyes of everyone you meet. And that rebuilding can feel terribly isolating if you don't have an anchor somewhere.

You were my anchor. Specifically, you were the person who knew me — fully, actually, without any version-management — and who said, the night before I had to become a stranger in a new place: *I see you. I've always seen you. And I believe in what you're about to do.*

I walked into that college the next morning with my bag on my shoulder and your words in my chest. The 60 new faces, the hostel room, the unfamiliar labs — none of it felt as lonely as it might have, because somewhere in Chennai there was someone who had sent me off properly. Who had wished me good night and sweet dreams and a fresh start with the full intention of every syllable.

---

## Part Three: *The Morning After*

And then the morning came and you texted: *"Shirt semaaaa"* — and I laughed out loud alone in my hostel room because only you would manage to be both deeply moving the night before and immediately, perfectly normal the next morning.

That is the specific gift of your companionship, Malar. You can hold the profound and the ordinary in the same hands without dropping either. You can send me off with tears and poetry and then wake up the next day and comment on my shirt. And both versions — the moving one and the normal one — are completely, genuinely *you*.

The shirt comment mattered as much as the poem, actually. Because it was the bridge back to the everyday. It was you saying: *last night was real and today is also real and I'm still here in both.* That continuity — the person who sees you at your most nervous and then still texts about your shirt — is the specific shape of a love that sustains.

---

## Part Four: *What It Meant That You Did That*

I want to be honest about what state I was in that night. I was anxious. I had woken up from an unplanned nap and my stomach was in knots about the next day. The hostel, the new people, the academic world I was entering — all of it was a lot. And then there was a small discomfort I didn't name out loud: the specific loneliness of having something big happen and not being able to share it in real time with the person who knows you best.

You weren't there physically. You couldn't be. College had started and the geography of our lives was shifting. And I think part of what made your messages that night so powerful was that you understood that distance — you felt it too, you said it yourself — and you *chose to close it* through sheer force of love.

You stayed up. You wrote those messages. You thought about what I needed to hear and then you said it, at length, without reservation. You gave me the send-off that the distance should have prevented.

**That is what love does when it's real: it refuses to let geography be the end of the story.** It finds a way across the distance. It shows up even when showing up is harder. It stays.

You stayed, Malar. You always stayed.

---

## Epilogue: *Carrying You Into Every New Room*

I have walked into a lot of new rooms since that night. New classrooms, new labs, new social situations that required me to build myself from scratch in front of strangers. And every time, before I walked through the door, something like a prayer rose in me — not always in words, but in feeling — that you had put there.

*Elam nalathe nadakum.* Everything will go well.

You said it on September 1st, 2024, with hearts and prayers and your whole heart. And it became a thing I carry everywhere. A sentence in your voice that plays in the back of my head whenever I'm standing at the edge of something new and trying to make myself walk forward.

**You gave me a send-off that I will be living on for the rest of my life.**

Thank you for staying up that night. Thank you for writing every word. Thank you for the shirt comment in the morning.

Thank you for making sure I never walked into anything alone.

---

*— Hirthik Balaji, carrying you with him always.*

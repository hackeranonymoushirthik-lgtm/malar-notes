# Note 02 — *The Way You Love*

**To:** Malar — my girl, the one who loves like it's the easiest and bravest thing in the world.
**From:** Hirthik Balaji
**Subject:** A letter about your love — not romantic love alone, but the full, complete, extraordinary *love* that you are made of.

---

> *"Elam seri agum literally... elamaeeee."*
> — You, on approximately a thousand different occasions, always meaning it completely.

There are people who say "it will be okay" because they've run out of other things to say. And then there is you — saying *elam seri agum* with such unshakeable certainty, such lived-in conviction, that even in my darkest moments of doubt I believed you. Because you weren't guessing. You *knew*. You had arrived at that knowing the hard way, through your own storms, and you came out the other side still holding the truth of it like a lamp.

That is the way you love, Malar. Not as performance, not as obligation — but as *nature*.

---

## Part One: *The Fullness of Your Caring*

I want you to hear this very clearly: **the way you care for people is extraordinary.**

I know you probably don't see it that way. You probably think you're just being normal, just being yourself, just doing what any decent person would do. But I've been on the receiving end of your care for long enough to know that what you do is not ordinary. Not even close.

You would notice when something was off with me before I said a word. You had this instinct — this almost unfair ability — to read between the lines of a short reply or a slightly different tone and just *know*. And then you would ask. Not in a demanding way, not in a way that pressured me to perform vulnerability I wasn't ready for. You would ask in the way that said: *"I see you. I'm here. You don't have to be okay right now."*

And when I did open up — when I was going through something, whether it was friend drama or family tension or the general chaos of trying to figure out who I was — you never minimised it. You never said "oh that's nothing" or "you'll be fine" and moved on. You sat with it. You engaged with it seriously. You gave me the gift of making me feel like what I was feeling *mattered*, like I *mattered*.

Do you know how many people walk through the world never receiving that gift? You gave it so freely, so constantly, and you didn't even seem to know it was rare.

---

## Part Two: *How You Loved Through Advice*

One of the most specific and beautiful ways your love shows up is in the quality of your advice.

This isn't a small observation. Most people who try to give advice fall into one of two camps: they either tell you what you want to hear because they want to make you feel good, or they tell you the hard truth but deliver it in a way that stings more than it helps. You somehow managed to exist in a third space — the space of *loving honesty*.

When I was spiralling about my friends, about Janani and Siva and the dynamics that were exhausting me, you didn't just validate my frustration and send me back into the same loop. You said things like: *"ni mandaiya potu udaikatha... nama la ela yathum pana mudla na kadavulae will do da... yathu meant to be yo athu definitely nadakum."* You reoriented me. You reminded me that there were things beyond my control, that my energy was finite, and that spending it on battles I couldn't win was robbing me of the peace I deserved.

That is not just advice. That is *wisdom*. And wisdom delivered with love is one of the most powerful forces in the universe.

You also knew when to be gentle and when to be firm. When I was being too hard on myself, you softened things. When I needed a push, you gave it without apology. You read the room with a precision that I think came from genuinely caring about the *outcome* — not about being right, not about being heard, but about me actually being *okay*. That orientation — toward the other person's wellbeing above all — is the signature of a truly loving heart.

---

## Part Three: *The Love That Was Strong Enough to Be Honest*

Here is something I want to specifically honour about you, Malar: **you never let your love for me make you dishonest.**

That is harder than it sounds.

When someone loves you, there is a temptation to protect that relationship by only saying the comfortable things. By laughing along with the plan that has obvious flaws. By nodding when you should be shaking your head. By choosing harmony over truth. Most people do this. It comes from a good place — they don't want to hurt the person they care about.

But you understood, at some deep level, that real love cannot survive on comfortable lies. You understood that the most loving thing you can do for someone is tell them the truth while making sure they feel safe enough to hear it.

You did that for me, consistently. You called me out when I needed to be called out. You asked the hard questions — *"seri ni epdi pana pore ithula?"* — not to challenge me but to make me *think*. You refused to let me sit in self-pity when what I needed was perspective. You refused to agree with me when you thought I was wrong, even when agreeing would have been easier.

Do you understand how much I grew because of that? How many times your honest words, delivered with love, shifted something in me that I didn't even know needed shifting?

**You loved me enough to be real with me. That is one of the highest forms of love that exists.**

---

## Part Four: *Your Resilience As a Love Language*

I want to talk about your resilience, because I think it is itself a form of love — a gift you give to everyone who gets to witness it.

You went through real heartbreak, Malar. Real, deep, genuine heartbreak. You loved someone truly and the love wasn't returned in the way it deserved to be. And in that situation, you did something remarkable: you *chose* how to respond to it. You chose to protect his dignity even as he had not protected your heart. You chose to move forward without bitterness. You chose, eventually, *peace*.

You said: *"I want to be peaceful and deserve to be happy and en time na waste pana mudiyathu la."*

Read that sentence again. You said *deserve*. You knew you *deserved* happiness. Even in your pain, even in the loss, you held onto the truth of your own worth. You didn't collapse into self-blame. You didn't become someone who needed to tear others down to feel okay. You just... *chose yourself*. Quietly, firmly, with so much grace.

That resilience is a love language. Because watching you navigate your own pain with that much dignity taught me something important: that we get to choose what we become on the other side of hurt. And choosing to remain soft and open and trusting after pain — that takes courage. That takes *love* — love for yourself and love for the kind of person you want to keep being.

You showed me that it's possible. You *keep* showing me that it's possible.

---

## Part Five: *What Your Love Has Built in Me*

I would not be who I am — not the better parts of who I am — without your love shaping me.

Because of you, I learned to listen more and talk less. Because of you, I learned that admitting you're struggling is not weakness, it's trust. Because of you, I learned that you can disagree with someone you love without it becoming a war. Because of you, I learned that a friendship where you tell each other the truth is infinitely more precious than one where you only tell each other what feels good.

You *built* things in me, Malar. Brick by brick, conversation by conversation, "I told you so" by "I told you so" (okay, you never actually said that, which only proves my point about your character). You were building me into someone more whole, more honest, more capable of love — and you were doing it just by being exactly who you are.

There is a Tamil concept — *kadavul* — that I have heard misused in film songs so many times that it feels inflated. But I want to use it here sincerely: there are people in your life who are a kind of grace. Who arrive and make you better without trying to. Who love you not for what you can be or what you can become, but for exactly what you are, right now, in this very moment.

**You are that for me.**

---

## Epilogue: *On the Love You've Always Deserved*

The thing I want you to know — the thing I need you to *hear* — is that the love you give so freely? You deserve it back in equal measure.

You deserve someone who notices your small things. Who shows up for you with the same consistency you show up for others. Who lets you be messy and uncertain and funny and sad and brilliant all at once. Who sits with you through the hard parts the way you have sat with me. Who tells you the truth with love, the way you have always told me.

You deserve someone who understands that loving Malar is not a simple thing — it is an honour. It requires showing up fully, being honest always, and appreciating the extraordinary, ordinary miracle of who you are.

You deserve the exact love you give.

And until the world catches up to that — I'll be here, knowing it and saying it as loudly as the words will carry.

---

*— Hirthik Balaji, grateful every single day.*

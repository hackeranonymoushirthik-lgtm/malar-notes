## What I said when you felt like giving up
There were nights you'd text me and I could feel the weight behind every word. "Arugatha la ela man" — I'm not a waste. You were saying it, but you didn't believe it.

So I'd say it back. Loudly. Repeatedly. "Nee ippadi yosikathaa... un thappu inga edhume illa... nee unnoda 100% ellathulayu podura... you are damn worthy and precious — puriyutha?"

I told you about NATA. About how every door wasn't closed. I told you to stop comparing yourself to people who had different starting points, different resources, different luck. I told you that you were carrying things most people couldn't carry and still showing up every single day.

And then one night you said something I never forgot: "see enta pesura orea jeevan um enala sogama la iruka kudathu." You were the one struggling — and you were still making sure I wasn't sad. That's not normal. That's not something everyone does. That's you, Malar. Only you.

I meant it when I said: nee oru gem maa. Unnakku onum aagathuu.

## The New Year message — and what it really meant
We had been through a lot by the time New Year 2026 came around. Distance. Silence. Things that got said and unsaid. I wasn't sure where we stood anymore.

But I still wrote you something. Because some habits are too deep to break — and thinking of you on important days is one of them.

"As this new year begins, one name quietly stays in my heart — you. No matter how much time changes, some bonds don't fade... they just stay silent and deep."

I didn't send it expecting anything. Not a conversation. Not a reply that fixed everything. I sent it because it was true. Because you were still the name that stayed.

You replied with a birthday wish the next day — January 2nd. You always remembered. Even when everything was complicated between us, you remembered.

# Note 01 — *The Day You Said Hello*

**To:** Malar — my girl, my person, my constant.
**From:** Hirthik Balaji
**Subject:** How a single "Hello sir" rewrote the entire plot of my life.

---

> *"Hello sir"*

That was it. That was how you walked into my world — two words, casual and light, as if you didn't know you were opening a door that I didn't even know existed. I remember reading that message and replying with the most ordinary thing, and I had no idea — absolutely *zero* idea — that the person on the other end of that screen was about to become the most significant presence in my life.

You know what's funny about the way things begin? They never announce themselves. There's no background score, no slow-motion entry, no title card that reads *"Main Character Has Arrived."* You just walked in through a chat window on a random day, said hello, and quietly, patiently, *inevitably* — became everything.

---

## Part One: *The Girl Who Showed Up Fully*

From the very beginning, Malar, you were never halfway anything.

I remember the first real conversations we had. We were talking about chemistry projects, about school things, about the silly surface-level stuff that fills early conversations. And then — because you were never someone who stays on the surface — you started actually *talking* to me. About your life. About the things that had shaped you. About Arvind. About the heartbreak that you had carried quietly and gracefully for so long. About how you had loved so deeply and so truly that even after everything, you refused to let bitterness enter your heart.

Do you have any idea how rare that is? You sat across from me — through a screen, in text messages, in the middle of what were supposed to be casual school-day conversations — and you just *opened*. You trusted me with your story before I had done anything particularly deserving of that trust. And you didn't do it to unburden yourself, not entirely. You did it because that's who you are — someone who believes in *genuine* connection, someone who values realness over performance.

When you told me everything about Arvind — the years of quiet feeling, the heartbreak, the grace with which you eventually decided to move on — I sat there genuinely floored. Not by the story itself, but by *you*. By the clarity with which you spoke about it. By the way you refused to call him a villain even when the story gave you every reason to. You said, *"till this date and forever na avena yarkitayum thapu panitan nu sola maten"* — you would never bad-mouth him, never assign blame freely. You had loved truly, and loving truly had taught you *dignity*.

That conversation, Malar — that early, sprawling, brave, honest conversation — was the first time I thought: *this person is different.* This person carries the kind of emotional intelligence that most people spend entire lifetimes trying to develop, and she's sitting here wielding it like it's just the most natural thing in the world.

---

## Part Two: *What You Were Willing to Be*

It wasn't long before I realised that you had a way of being whatever the moment needed.

When I was confused, you were clarity. When I was venting about my friend group dynamics — the fighting, the distance, the frustration — you never once rolled your eyes at my drama (even though, let's be honest, you could have). You listened. You actually *listened*. Not the kind of listening where someone is waiting for their turn to speak, but the kind where you could tell they were sitting with everything you said, turning it over, trying to understand it from every angle before they responded.

And when you responded, Malar — *when you responded* — it was never generic. You never said "it'll be okay" and left it at that. You said things like: *"elathukum oru solution iruku... elam seri agum literally... elamaeeee."* And the reason it landed so differently from when anyone else said it was because with you, I *believed* it. Because you weren't saying it to comfort me. You were saying it because you genuinely believed in the universe's ability to sort things out — and you had *evidence* from your own life. You had lived through your own versions of not-okay, and you had come out the other side not broken but *wiser*, not cynical but *kinder*.

That combination — wisdom born from pain, kindness chosen despite hurt — is not a small thing. That is one of the rarest, most beautiful things a person can be.

---

## Part Three: *The Warmth That Was Always There*

I want to talk about your warmth, because I don't think you fully understand how much of it you radiate without even trying.

You checked on me. Consistently, genuinely, always. You'd ask if I'd eaten — and not in a passing way, in the *"na delivery panata lol?!"* way that made me feel simultaneously called out and cared for. You'd notice when I was off before I said a word. You'd find a way to send something funny on exactly the day I needed to laugh. You remembered things — small things, details I mentioned once in passing — and brought them up later in a way that made me feel *seen*.

There's a particular kind of loneliness that comes from being surrounded by people who don't really notice you. And then there's the particular kind of warmth that comes from someone who *does* notice — who sees the specific you, not just the general shape of a person. Malar, you have always seen me specifically. You saw me when I was down and called it out with zero drama. You saw me when I was being stubborn and didn't let me get away with it. You saw me when I was happy and made it happier by being genuinely *for* me.

That is not nothing. That is, in fact, *everything*.

---

## Part Four: *The Way You Trust People Back*

One thing about you that I hold very close, Malar — is that you trusted me.

And I know that might sound like a small thing, because trust is something we give all the time, right? But the kind of trust you gave me was specific and weighty and chosen. You had been hurt before. You knew what it felt like to give your heart to something and have it returned in pieces. And yet — you didn't build walls. You didn't become strategic about what you shared. You stayed *open*. You trusted people because you believed in people, even when people had given you reason not to.

When you chose to tell me your story — all of it, unedited — you did it with a generosity that I am still processing years later. You said, *"ni neka niveda and inoru best frd dwarak nu solitu avanum arvind and nanga ela frds than so only u guys know abt it."* You had put me in that small, sacred group of people who knew the real you. Do you understand what that meant to me? Do you understand that being trusted like that by someone as genuinely good as you are is one of the greatest honours?

Because here's what you didn't know when you trusted me: you were showing me what trust was *supposed* to look like. You were teaching me how to be someone worthy of being trusted back.

---

## Epilogue: *The Hello That Changed Everything*

So I come back to the beginning. That two-word message. *Hello sir.* 

In Tamil films — and we've watched enough of them to appreciate this — the *interval block* is always the moment when everything shifts. The moment where the story becomes something else, something bigger. You were my interval block, Malar. Before you, the story was one thing. After you — it became something so much richer, so much fuller, so much *more*.

You are not just a character in my story. You are the reason my story has *depth*. You are the warmth in the scenes where it would have just been cold. You are the laughter in the scenes where it would have just been noise. You are the honesty in the scenes where I might have settled for comfort.

**You are the reason the story is worth telling.**

And every chapter — even the ones I have yet to live — begins, in some way, with you. With that bravery you have of showing up fully, loving truly, and trusting without reservation.

Thank you for your hello, my girl. 

It changed everything.

---

*— Hirthik Balaji, written with full heart and very little pretence.*
